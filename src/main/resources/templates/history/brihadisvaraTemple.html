A Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva located in south bank of Kaveri river in Thanjavur, TN, India. Built by Raja Raja Chola I between 1003 and 1010 AD, the temple is a part of UNESCO World Heritage Site and exemplary example of a fully realized Dravidian architecture.

It is also famed for the quality of its sculpture, as well as being the location that commissioned the brass Nataraja - Shiva. The complex includes shrines of other deities part of Shaivism, Vaishnavism and Shaktism traditions of Hinduism.
