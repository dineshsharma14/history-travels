Chola History Trail

The Cholas were not just one of the longest ruling dynasties in the Indian history, they were also more of a phenomenon; fore-fathers of so many innovations - in water management for a predominantly agrarian society, in levying relevant taxes and composing intelligent social laws for an expanding wealthy population.

Travelling with us, you will experience the grandeur of Cholas, walk through their battlefields which led to their meteoric rise, stroll across the ruins which mourn their unpropitious fall, the drama and intrigue behind their strategic alliances and their final resting places. If you are someone willing to understand and see the golden past, we are waiting.

A Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva located in south bank of Kaveri river in Thanjavur, TN, India. Built by Raja Raja Chola I between 1003 and 1010 AD, the temple is a part of UNESCO World Heritage Site and exemplary example of a fully realized Dravidian architecture.

This museum was built by the State of Tamil Nadu in 1984 to celebrate the 1000th birth anniversary of the great Chola king Raja Raja Chola. King Raja Raja Chola was the most famous King in the Chola dynasty that ruled Thanjavur.

The Sri Ranganathaswamy Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to Ranganatha, a reclining form of the Lord Vishnu. Constructed in the Dravidian architectural style, the temple is glorified by Alvars in Divya Prabhanda and has unique distinction of being not only the foremost among 108 Divya Desams dedicated to Vishnu, but also the largest functioning Hindu temple in the world.
